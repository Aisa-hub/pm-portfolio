About

Hi, I'm Aissatou.

I graduated from Columbia University in May 2026 with a degree in Computer Science and Public Health, and a passion for building products that solve real problems for real people. My background sits at the intersection of technical depth and human impact, which shapes how I approach every product decision, from defining success metrics to shipping features that users actually need.

I have had the opportunity to work across both big tech enterprise and startup environments. At Amazon, I drove data pipeline optimization and delivered self-serve analytics tools across 14 global marketplaces. At Sachi AI Health, I owned the full PM lifecycle: conducting user research, prioritizing the roadmap, defining engagement metrics, and shipping product. At MetLife, I translated competitive market data into pricing strategy recommendations that reached stakeholders across the business. Each experience sharpened my ability to move fast, communicate clearly, and build with intention.

Outside of work, I am someone who sees a gap and acts. When I noticed new migrants in my community without warm clothing last winter, I organized a donation drive across two local mosques in Queens and got it done. That same instinct drives my product work: find the problem, bring the right people together, and build something that actually reaches the people who need it.
